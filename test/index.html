The Challenge

Most HOAs aren't ready for WUCIOA

Washington's new HOA law is the most sweeping in the nation — and the compliance burden falls squarely on volunteer board members who are already stretched thin.

📄

No central document hub

WUCIOA requires associations to maintain extensive records and respond to requests within 10 business days. Without a website, boards are buried in one-off emails and paper requests.

⏰

The clock is ticking

Governing documents that conflict with WUCIOA become automatically void on January 1, 2028. The restatement process takes 6–18 months. Communities that wait until 2027 risk missing the deadline.

💰

Litigation exposure

There's no state enforcement agency — it's all private lawsuits with bilateral fee-shifting. Non-compliant actions can be challenged and invalidated within 90 days. The cost of defense far exceeds the cost of compliance.

Understanding WUCIOA

The timeline every Washington HOA needs to know

WUCIOA (RCW 64.90) is replacing all prior HOA statutes in Washington. Here's what's already happened and what's coming.

July 1, 2018
WUCIOA enacted
Applies in full to every community created on or after this date. Budget ratification procedures apply to all associations.
June 2024
ESSB 5796 — the major overhaul
Washington becomes the first state to adopt the 2021 UCIOA framework. The statute grows to 67,000+ words. Phases in full applicability for all communities by 2028.
January 1, 2026 — Now in effect
Cross-apply provisions kick in
Open meetings, owner comment periods, voting rules, assessments, reserve studies, EV charger rights, heat pump rights, and updated foreclosure procedures now apply to all Washington HOAs.
Now through 2027
The compliance window
Associations should be restating governing documents, updating insurance, obtaining reserve studies, and training boards. This process takes 6–18 months — don't wait.
January 1, 2028
Full applicability — old laws repealed
RCW 64.32, 64.34, 64.38, and 58.19 are repealed. Every residential community falls under WUCIOA. Conflicting governing documents become automatically void. No grace period.

Important note: NW HOA Portal provides opinions and guidance on WUCIOA readiness — we are not attorneys and do not provide legal advice. Our assessments are informational only. For legal counsel on governing document amendments, compliance obligations, or dispute resolution, we recommend consulting a qualified community association attorney.
